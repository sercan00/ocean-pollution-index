Key Findings

What the data reveals

A few of the most striking results from the index — explore the full dashboard to see all 30 regions.

🌊
Indian Ocean tops the index
208 million people live within 10km of its shores, combined with the highest microplastic concentrations of any ocean basin. It carries the heaviest human pressure on Earth.
Score: 48.4 / 100
🌏
North Pacific close behind
The North Pacific records the lowest mean ocean pH of any basin (8.024) and 118 million coastal residents — a combination of acidification and population pressure.
Score: 47.8 / 100
🏞️
The Great Lakes rival oceans
Lake-wide microplastic densities reach into the hundreds of thousands of particles per km² — pushing the Great Lakes above most ocean basins on the index.
Score: 43.2 / 100
🐟
South China Sea accelerating
A mean of 1,198 microplastic items per m³ in the central basin, fed by major river input from the Mekong and Pearl Rivers, makes this one of the most plastic-dense seas measured.
Score: 41.6 / 100
🧊
Arctic still low — for now
The Arctic scores low today, but it's warming faster than any region on the index — roughly 0.02°C per year under the IPCC mid-range scenario. A future hotspot in the making.
Score: 6.4 / 100
📉
Some regions improve
The Mediterranean is one of the few water bodies projected to improve — a declining measured plastic trend and shrinking coastal population pull its 2100 forecast downward, not up.
Improving by 2100
